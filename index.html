Catchies by Fear

“Present fears are less than horrible imaginings.”, Shakespear

The deprivation of meaning terrifies man because it deprives him of his reference points, throwing him into the unknown. The same applies to the saturation of the senses. Once out of his comfort zone, it is enough to shock him by varying between restriction and saturation.

This is how Jumpscare was born. Sudden visual and audio appearances that will startle the viewer into a cold sweat. This is the preferred and most well-known mechanism of horror workers. However, be careful not to overuse it, as a game relying on this mechanic is bound to be a bad game.

The user must feel all control slipping away, as if thrown into the middle of the sea with no landmarks or land on the horizon, devoid of any anchorage to what they know. This places the user directly in a different world, a dangerous and terrifying world.

Putting the spectator on the edge of his seat is very important in horror. This permanent tension tends to put the viewer in danger in a situation where every minute of rest is a harbinger of an imminent threat. This feeling of insecurity often materialises in the form of a danger from which there is no escape (Halloween, It Follows, Slenderman and Devour)
